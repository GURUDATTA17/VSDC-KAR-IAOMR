Abstract guidelines (Scientific Paper/Poster )

  • Registration and participation in person are mandatory for all the events.
  • One delegate can present both paper and poster if interested.
  • One student can present 1 paper and two students can present 1 poster.
  • The Abstract, Poster or paper should not be plagiarized as this can lead to rejection of the abstract/paper/poster.
  • Time allotted
      Paper presentation – 8 min (6 min for presentation and 2 min for question and answer session)
      Poster presentation – 5 min (3 min for presentation and 2 min for question and answer session)
  • Strict adherence to the time limit should be followed ; failing to do so will disqualify you from the competitive category.
  • The final decision regarding the papers or posters will be sole decision of the scientific team.

  • Last date for abstract submission for the delegate paper or poster – 25th March 2024 (till 5pm)
  • Last date for submission of final E-poster – 25th March 2024 (till 5pm)

    Guidelines for paper presentation

    Please prepare both PowerPoint (compatible with PowerPoint 2013 version) and PDF version. Since the display size for your presentation may be variable, please follow the guidelines mentioned below to have a nice presentation session. Both the versions of your presentation should be carried in person by you in a USB drive and handed over to the person in charge before the beginning of their respective sessions. The presentation will be allowed for 8 mins followed by 2mins for discussion.

  • Text

    The text should be clear, to the point, and legible from about 10 feet away. The title should be short and draw interest, with font size legible from approximately 2–3 meters. The body text should be legible from 6 feet away. The authors must submit the title and an abstract containing not more than 200 words, along with their names, guides names, college address for their paper before 30th March 2024. Each paper can have only one registered delegate presenting it.

  • Abstract Format:

  • Use Times New Roman or Arial font, 12-point size.
  • Limit the abstract length to 200 words.
  • Include the following sections when relevant
      Introduction/Objective
      Methods
      Results
      Conclusion
  • Do not include references or acknowledgments in the abstract.

    • Guidelines for E Poster presentation

  • Content

    The poster should be concise and contain the essential details to explain in 4 minutes. The poster should not carry any name, identity details of the presenters. A poster number will be given before the session and the presenter may identify themselves based on the number while presenting.

  • Design

    The poster should be clean, organized, and simple. It should have a consistent layout with consistent margins, line spacing, color, style, and shading. The poster should use minimal text, with figures and tables being the main focus. Please prepare both PowerPoint (compatible with PowerPoint 2013 version) and PDF version. Since the display size for your E poster may be variable, please follow the guidelines mentioned below to have a nice presentation session. The poster should not be more than 10MB in size and is to be carried in person. Both the versions of your presentation should be carried in person by you in a USB drive and handed over to the person in charge before the beginning of their respective sessions.

  • Text

    The text should be clear, to the point, and legible from about 10 feet away. The title should be short and draw interest, with font size legible from approximately 2–3 meters. The body text should be legible from 6 feet away. • Graphics The poster should include text and graphics, and use color to add emphasis and clarity. Illustrations should be simple and bold, and photos should clearly show pertinent details.The authors must submit the title and an abstract containing not more than 200 words, along with their names, guides names, college address for their E-poster before 30th March 2024. Each poster can have not more than two registered delegates presenting it.

  • Abstract Format:

  • Use Times New Roman or Arial font, 12-point size.
  • Limit the abstract length to 200 words.
  • Do not include references or acknowledgments in the abstract.
  • Include the following sections when relevant
      Introduction/Objective
      Methods
      Results
      Conclusion

    • Guidelines for abstract submission

    The author’s identity and college name should not be there.

  • The abstract for the paper and poster presentation should be submitted in either of the three categories namely original research, case report and review.
  • Abstracts should not exceed 250 words (excusing references)
  • Maximum of two references are allowed.
  • Images, graphs and tables are not allowed in the abstract.
  • Kindly maintain uniform font size of Time new roman 12 for the content and 14 for the heading.
  • A structure abstract in the format – (Introduction, Material and Methods, Results, Discussion and Conclusion) should be submitted for original research.
  • Title should be clear.
  • The E-poster will be scrutinized by our panel of judges and only the finally selected 10 E-posters will be allowed to present at the venue.
